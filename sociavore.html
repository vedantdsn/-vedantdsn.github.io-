Sociavore Rebrand

In Summer 2020, I worked as a Product Design Intern at Sociavore, a start-up focused on helping restaurants establish an online presence and improve their online experience. Over the course of three months, my biggest project was the rebranding of Sociavore.

DURATION

June 2020 - September 2020

ROLE

Art Director and Product Designer

TEAM

Me + 2 Project Managers & Developer

OUR TASK

How might we make Sociavore’s website more approachable to attract more customers to the platform?

FINAL PRODUCT

The rebranded website has launched in November 2020. There were a few changes made even after this case study was made.

Check out the project here

CURRENT FLOW

We began our process by exploring the current navigation of the current Sociavore website to get a better understanding of the product. We noticed that there was a lot of confusion when trying to go through the feature pages. The lack of organization with the information made it difficult to identify the features. Apart from the navigation bar, the website had no clear call to action for the user to sign up or explore other pages.

USER RESEARCH

In order to understand the website experience from an outsider's perspective, we had Sociavore’s current customers and first-time site visitors to navigate through the website and ask for their opinion on their way.

PERSONA

With our primary and secondary research, we formed a persona for the project.

USER JOURNEY

Using a customer journey map, we focused on the Consideration phase. This stage is the most important as it is where the customer is exploring the website and deciding to sign up or not.

HOME PAGE ITERATIONS

We went through multiple rounds of explorations of the landing and feature pages. Focusing on the landing page, we began by creating low-fidelity wireframes. Goal was to keep it simple but also aesthetically pleasing to encourage the user to stay on the website. These iterations were presented to the project manager and rest of the team.

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

From the user feedback, we revisited the Information Architecture of the content within the feature pages. In order to fix the confusion with Sociavore’s features, we organized all of them into three main categories: Build, Grow, and Connect. Each category got it’s own landing page that explained and linked to a separate feature.

CARD BASED UI

A Card based User Interface was implemented as they’re visually appealing, and easy to digest for users. Cards work well with different screen sizes and also are editable.

FINAL PRODUCT

The rebranded website has launched in November 2020. There were a few changes made even after this case study was made.
